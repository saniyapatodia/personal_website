Executive Summary

We are building India's first end-to-end, medically supervised weight loss program powered by GLP-1 medications (Mounjaro and Ozempic).

Core positioning: “Lose 10-15% body weight with full medical support - safely, simply, and sustainably.”

GLP-1 is the hook. The program is the product. Trust is the moat.

Market Opportunity

Scale of the Problem

  • ~23-24% of Indian adults (15-49 years) are overweight or obese
  • Urban India is substantially higher - studies show 40-50%+ in some cities
  • ~71% of Indians are metabolically unhealthy - including many who appear thin
  • Up to 40% of women have central (abdominal) obesity

The “Skinny Fat” Problem

India has a large underserved segment: people who appear thin but carry high body fat and poor metabolic health. This dramatically expands our addressable market.

Our Real Market (not just obese people)

  • Overweight urban professionals
  • Women with PCOS and hormonal weight issues
  • Individuals with insulin resistance or pre-diabetes
  • The metabolically unhealthy segment

Total addressable population: 150-200 million people in India, with strong willingness to pay among the urban upper-middle class.

Why this is hard

  • GLP-1 drugs are expensive in India, limiting near-term market to upper-middle class
  • Regulation is strict - non-compliant fulfilment or aggressive marketing can shut you down

Full Operating Model

Step 1 - Patient Acquisition

Patient acquisition in India requires a trust-first approach rather than a direct response model. Primary channels are WhatsApp-based referrals, doctor partnerships, and YouTube health content. Instagram is effective for urban women aged 22-35 but should be treated as an awareness channel, not a conversion channel. Corporate wellness partnerships offer a high-conversion B2B path into the target income segment.

Homepage headline: “Lose 10-15% body weight with medical support - India's most structured weight loss program.”

A 2-3 minute intake quiz collects:

  • Current weight, height, BMI estimate
  • Medical history (PCOS, thyroid, diabetes, medications)
  • Weight loss goals and timeline

Step 2 - Eligibility Engine

The quiz feeds a rules-based classifier:

  • ❌ Not eligible - directed to lifestyle resources
  • ⚠️ Needs doctor review - flagged for consultation
  • ✅ Likely eligible - fast-tracked to consultation booking

⚠️ The platform never prescribes. Eligibility is indicative only - all clinical decisions are made by a licensed doctor.

Step 3 - Doctor Consultation (Mandatory)

Every patient must complete a medical consultation before any prescription is issued. Doctors are Indian MBBS/MD-qualified with telehealth certification.

  • Patient completes an async medical form
  • Optional video call (doctor may request a quick call if needed)
  • Doctor decides: lifestyle-only plan, GLP-1 prescription, or rejection

Step 4 - Prescription and Fulfilment

The doctor generates a legal prescription. Medication delivery is handled by licensed pharmacy partners - we do not ship directly.

  • Partner pharmacies: Tata 1mg, PharmEasy
  • Pharmacies verify prescription and manage delivery
  • We are the program layer, not the pharmacy

Step 5 - The Program Layer (Core Product)

Patients log:

  • Weight (weekly)
  • Appetite and hunger levels
  • Side effects (nausea, dizziness, fatigue)

Platform delivers - Insight, Trade-off, Action:

InsightTrade-offAction
High appetite suppression - protein intake at risk Faster fat loss vs. muscle loss risk Add one high-protein meal daily

Step 6 - Safety Monitoring and Escalation

  • Severe nausea - doctor alert
  • Dizziness or fainting - immediate escalation
  • Weight plateau beyond threshold - protocol adjustment
  • All serious flags route back to the prescribing doctor

Step 7 - Retention and Engagement Loop

GLP-1 dropout peaks in months 1-3, exactly when side effects are worst and patients feel most uncertain. The response to that is human contact, not push notifications.

Scheduled Calls (Core of Retention)

  • Monthly doctor call (15-20 min) - reviews weight progress, adjusts dosage, addresses side effects clinically.
  • Fortnightly health coach call (10-15 min) - motivation, habit reinforcement, handles non-clinical concerns.
  • On-demand urgent call (within 24 hours) - safety net for side effects, anxiety, or dropout risk.

Async Touchpoints (Between Calls)

  • Weekly check-in via WhatsApp or in-app form
  • Personalised feedback based on check-in responses, delivered same day
  • Progress dashboard with milestone tracking
  • Milestone nudges and motivational triggers

Why Calls Are Non-Negotiable

Real-world retention data shows that patients who had more frequent provider contact were significantly more likely to complete 12 weeks of GLP-1 treatment. In India specifically, patients taking an expensive, unfamiliar injectable medication need ongoing reassurance from a real person to stay on program. A dashboard alone will not hold them.

WhatsApp is the primary async channel. Calls are the primary retention mechanism. The program should feel like having a doctor and a coach in your pocket, not a subscription app.

Phase 2

  • Peer community access (WhatsApp group or in-app) for patients who have completed 3 months

Execution Plan

Phase 1 - Validation (Weeks 1-3)

Goal: 100+ signups. If not achieved, reposition before building anything.

Build

  • Simple landing page with a 2-3 minute intake quiz
  • WhatsApp number as the primary CTA - not just an email form
  • Email waitlist as secondary capture only

Acquire

  • Instagram content targeting urban women aged 22-38 - PCOS/thyroid angles
  • YouTube - short doctor-led explainer builds trust fast
  • WhatsApp broadcast lists via personal networks and communities
  • Doctor referral network - 2-3 endocrinologists or GPs locally
  • Do not use Reddit - negligible reach for this audience in India

Validate before spending

  • Share content in relevant WhatsApp groups manually first
  • If it spreads organically, the message is working
  • Only pay to amplify what already works

Phase 2 - Manual MVP (Weeks 4-8)

Run the entire program manually. No tech required. Validate without building anything.

Patient flow (all on WhatsApp)

  • Patient signs up via landing page or WhatsApp directly
  • You send intake form via WhatsApp (Google Form link)
  • You review and connect them to the doctor over WhatsApp or Zoom
  • Doctor consults (async first, video for complex cases)
  • Doctor prescribes; patient orders from Tata 1mg or PharmEasy

Weekly check-in

  • Send a simple WhatsApp message every Monday: weight, appetite, side effects, how they’re feeling
  • Respond same day with personalised feedback
  • Flag anything concerning to the doctor immediately

Track everything in a Google Sheet

  • Patient name, start date, weight, weekly log, dropout reason if applicable
  • This data becomes your retention model and informs Phase 3

What to learn in this phase

  • Where do patients drop off and why?
  • What questions do they ask most on WhatsApp?
  • What does the doctor spend most time on?
  • What would a health coach do differently from the founder?

Phase 3 - Build Product (Post-Demand Confirmed)

Only build once you have 20+ paying patients and clear patterns from Phase 2.

What to build first (priority order)

  • WhatsApp automation for weekly check-ins (keep it feeling personal)
  • Simple patient dashboard - weight, side effects, progress
  • Doctor consultation booking system
  • Safety monitoring and escalation workflows

What to build later

  • Native app - unnecessary until 200+ patients
  • Email flows - WhatsApp works better for this demographic
  • Pharmacy API integration - manual ordering works early

Staffing at this stage

  • Part-time health coach (certified nutritionist/wellness coach, Rs 25,000-35,000/month)
  • Founder manages doctor relationships and escalations
  • One doctor on retainer can handle up to 50 patients

Legal and Compliance Framework

⚠️ This is a regulated healthcare space. Legal compliance is non-negotiable and must be built in from day one.

AreaRequirementAction
TelemedicineFollow Telemedicine Practice Guidelines IndiaDoctor onboarding + verification + consent flow
Prescription DrugsSchedule H - cannot sell without prescriptionPartner with licensed doctors + pharmacies
Doctor LiabilityDoctors responsible for prescriptionsClear terms separating platform vs doctor liability
Data PrivacyDPDP Act 2023 - health data is sensitiveExplicit consent, secure storage, no unauthorised sharing
Drug AdvertisingDrugs and Magic Remedies Act - no miracle claimsClinical, educational, risk-aware messaging only
Pharmacy DeliveryLicensed pharmacy required for dispensingPartner with Tata 1mg, PharmEasy

Recommended Legal Structure

  1. Your Startup (Platform) - matches users to doctors and pharmacies. Does not prescribe. Does not dispense.
  2. Doctors (Independent) - consult patients, make clinical decisions, issue prescriptions. Liability rests with them.
  3. Pharmacies (Licensed Partners) - verify prescriptions, dispense, and deliver medication.

🚨 Critical rule: You never directly prescribe or sell medication. The platform is an intermediary.

MVP Legal Approach

  • Phase 1: Education, eligibility screening, and doctor booking only - no drug sales
  • Phase 2: Add prescription flow and pharmacy integration with legal review

Recommended first steps: Engage one healthcare lawyer in India + one doctor who has prescribed GLP-1 medications.

Final Perspective

You are not building an Ozempic startup. You are not building a pharmacy.

You are building India's first clean, structured, medically supervised GLP-1 weight loss system - where patients feel:

“This is the easiest way to lose weight safely in India.”

This idea is feasible. It is not easy. But if executed correctly - with doctors front and centre, compliance built in, and a patient experience that is genuinely better than anything else available - it becomes a very defensible, very valuable business.
